Energy Harvesting: Synchrotron Radiation Recovery

1. From Waste to Power: The Alpha Mantle Utility

In high-energy particle accelerators like the LCC 91km, Synchrotron Radiation is typically a parasitic byproduct that causes significant heat load on cryogenic systems. The Alpha Mantle of the BMC-1 system is specifically engineered to intercept this radiation before it reaches the superconducting magnet core.

Radiation Capture: The internal layer of the Alpha Mantle absorbs high-energy photons emitted by the circulating beam.

Thermal Gradient: This absorption creates a controlled thermal gradient between the inner wall and the cooled Ruthenium layer.

2. Quantum Thermoelectric Conversion

The conversion of intercepted radiation into electrical power occurs within the Kagome lattice through integrated micro-thermoelectric modules and topological edge states.

Kagome Integration: The engineered triangles of the alpha-RuCl3 lattice are interleaved with nano-scale thermoelectric junctions that exploit the Seebeck effect at cryogenic scales.

Majorana Efficiency: Because the heat is transported via topologically protected Majorana Fermions, the energy loss during transport is minimal, allowing for a highly efficient conversion process.

Self-Scaling Cooling: A unique feature of this system is that higher beam intensities (which produce more radiation) automatically generate more power for the cooling mantle, making the system self-regulating.
