1. The Engineered Kagome Lattice

The physical realization of the BMC-1 system relies on the geometric arrangement of $\alpha-RuCl_3$ (Ruthenium Trichloride) atoms. While the material naturally forms a honeycomb lattice, we utilize strain-engineering to force it into a Kagome geometry (intercalated triangles and hexagons).

Magnetic Frustration: The Kagome lattice maximizes geometric frustration, which is the primary driver for stabilizing the Kitaev Quantum Spin Liquid state.

Chiral Heat Channels: This specific geometry creates "topologically protected" one-way streets for Majorana Fermions. This ensures that heat extracted from the $Nb_3Sn$ core is pumped longitudinally without the risk of back-scattering.

2. Atomic Layer Deposition (ALD) Protocol

To achieve the precise thickness of 150nm required for optimal 1.2 GHz synchronization, we employ Plasma-Enhanced Atomic Layer Deposition (PE-ALD). This process allows us to coat the magnet components atom-by-atom, ensuring perfect uniformity across the 91km ring.

Layer 1: Graphene Base (10nm): Acts as a structural anchor and a high-speed electron highway for the integrated sensors.

Layer 2: $\alpha-RuCl_3$ Active Mantle (130nm): The primary quantum engine where the Boghian-Shift entropy extraction occurs.

Layer 3: h-BN Insulation (10nm): A hexagonal Boron Nitride capping layer that provides electrical isolation while remaining thermally transparent to the cooling pulse.
